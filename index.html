Early Life and Education

All about his childhood

President Cleveland was born on March 18, 1837, in Caldwell, New Jersey. He was the son of Richard Falley and Ann Neal Cleveland. He grew up with 8 siblings. Due to financial struggles, he did not attend college but clerked at a law firm in Buffalo, New York. Notable events in his early life included his father’s death and his early legal career.

Election and Political Alignment

Cleveland was a Democrat and remains the only U.S. president to serve two non-consecutive terms. He stood for honesty, anti-corruption policies, and opposed excessive government spending and high taxes.

1884 Election: Ran against Republican James G. Blaine. Cleveland won the electoral vote 219 to 182 and won the popular vote by 0.5%.

1888 Election: Ran against Benjamin Harrison but lost the electoral vote (168 to 233) despite winning the popular vote (48.6%).

1892 Election: Defeated Harrison with an electoral vote of 277 and a popular vote of 46.0%.

Presidency and Life After Presidency

During his presidency

Cleveland fought against government corruption, regulated railroads to prevent unfair pricing, repealed the Sherman Silver Purchase Act, and lowered tariffs. His presidency also saw the Panic of 1893, labor strikes, and debates over the annexation of Hawaii.

He was nicknamed “Grover the Good” and remains the only president to serve two non-consecutive terms. After retiring to Princeton, New Jersey, he passed away on June 24, 1908, due to a heart attack.
